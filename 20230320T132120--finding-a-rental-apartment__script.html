4.2. Score metric

I don't want to spend time looking at +6700 apartment, so I will rank them using a metric and start from the highest scored apartment, since it is most likely to be a hit.

What variables should the score metric be base on?

  • Area: The area of the apartment, the bigger the better.
  • Pictures: The number of pictures the owner have posted to be reviewed.
  • Pieces: The number bedrooms and living areas, the more the better.
  • Stage: The floor number.
  • Price: The price of monthly rent, the lower the better.
  • Old: The buildings' age, the lower the better
  • Features_number: The total number of features a building/apartment have, the more the better.
    The features are: Air conditioning, Box room, Concierge, Double glazing, Elevator, Equipped kitchen, European lounge, Exterior facade, Fireplace, Flooring: Marble, Flooring: Tiled, Flooring: Wooden, Fridge, Furnished, Garage, Garden, Heating, Internet, Microwave, Moroccan lounge, Mountains views, Orientation: East, Orientation: North, Orientation: South, Orientation: West, Oven, Pets allowed, Pool, Reinforced Door, Satellite dish, Sea views, Security system, TV, Terrace, and Washing machine.
  • Places_number: The total number of places near the building/apartment, the more the better.
    Example: near the building there are 4 ATM's, 7 Cafe, 1 Hospital, 1 Store. the place number will be \(4+7+1+1=13\).
    The places are: ATM, bakery, cafe, dentist, doctor, gas_station, gym, hair_care, hospital, meal_delivery, meal_takeaway, park, pharmacy, physiotherapist, police, post_office, restaurant, school, shopping_mall, spa, store, supermarket, tourist_attraction, train_station, transit_station, and university.

\[Score=\frac{\log{(Area)}*\log{(Pictures+1)}*\log{(Pieces+1)}*\log{(Stage+1)}}{\log{(Price)}*\log{(Old)}}*\log{Features}*\log{Places}\]
